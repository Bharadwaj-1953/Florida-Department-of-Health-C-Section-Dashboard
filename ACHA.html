Key Takeaways
A quick read of the Florida Medicaid C-section data.
  • Primary C-section rates have gone down every year since 2017. The lowest rate in the data is in 2023.
  • Older mothers see higher primary C-section rates than younger mothers, in every year of the data.
  • Florida Medicaid regions show real differences. Some areas are consistently above the statewide average.
  • Things have improved overall, but the gaps across age and region have stayed in place over the eight years on record.
Reading this as a Medicaid recipient?

Here's what the data means for you.

These numbers describe real trends across hundreds of thousands of Florida Medicaid births. They're not a prediction for your pregnancy — but they can help you walk into your next prenatal visit with better questions.

  • ↓
    Good news overall. First-time C-section rates have fallen every year since 2017. The 2023 rate is the lowest in this dataset.
  • ↑
    Age is the biggest factor. Rates climb steadily past age 30. If you're older, knowing this helps you have an honest conversation with your provider about your birth plan.
  • ◎
    Where you deliver matters too. Rates vary by up to 10 percentage points across Florida regions, even for women the same age.

What is a primary C-section?

A primary cesarean section is a first-time surgical birth. It's a delivery done through an incision in the abdomen and uterus rather than vaginally. The first C-section matters most, because once a woman has had one, later pregnancies often involve another.

Florida Medicaid covers birth expenses for low-income pregnant women. AHCA tracks primary C-sections specifically (women who have had one before are not counted) so the agency can see where first-time surgical deliveries might be reduced through better prenatal care.

The statewide rate has fallen by about a fifth since 2017. The rate still varies a fair amount by a mother's age and by where in Florida she delivers.

Source: Florida Medicaid claims and encounter records. APR-DRG 540, 541, 542, 560 and CPT 59400 to 59622. Calendar years 2016 to 2023. Rows with fewer than 30 deliveries are excluded.

Rates go up with age, in every year
Primary C-section rates rise with the mother's age. Women age 40 and older had the highest rates in every year of the data. In 2023, that was 24.2%, compared to 15.6% for mothers under 20.

About a 10-point gap between the highest and lowest regions
In 2023, Region 11 (Miami-Dade and Monroe) had the highest primary C-section rate at 24.7%. Region 3 (North Central) had the lowest at 14.2%. A gap of about that size has shown up in every year since 2016.

For Florida Medicaid Recipients
What this means for you
  • Primary C-section rates on Florida Medicaid have come down since 2017.
  • Older mothers tend to have higher primary C-section rates than younger mothers.
  • Rates are different in different parts of Florida, even for women in the same age group.
  • Every pregnancy is different. The numbers on this page describe a whole population. They aren't a prediction for any one birth.
Talk with your healthcare provider about the delivery plan that's right for you. A few good questions to ask: Is a C-section being recommended for a medical reason? What are the alternatives? What does recovery usually look like?
